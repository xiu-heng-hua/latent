# E1 — Tone & creative color grading

Implements the Audit-03 tone/color-grading decisions (`../decisions/03-tone-and-color-grading.md`) plus the `point_curve` safety half of code-review CR-02 H1 (`../decisions/code-review/02-pipeline-and-edit-model.md`). The through-line: **move the grading core into perceptually-uniform spaces (L\*, LCh)** and make every curve total/monotone by construction.

**Depends on E0** — specifically **E0-C1** (the `Lab`/`LCh` module: working→XYZ→Lab→LCh + inverse, D50-referenced, and the exposed `L*` perceptual lightness). Do not re-implement that math here; consume it. E1-C1/C3/C6 also depend on E0's settled working space + output transform (E0-C3/C6) since L\* is computed via XYZ Y off the working primaries.

**Epic-wide GPU rule (Initiative G).** Three ops in this epic run on the GPU shader `latent-gpu/src/map_pixels.wgsl`: **tone** (`PointOp::Tone`, op 1) and **saturation** (`PointOp::Saturation`, op 2) — plus the highlight-headroom branch shared by tone. `Gain` is op 0. The other three ops (`Curves`, `ColorMix`, `Matrix`) are **CPU-only** today (the GPU backend falls back to `CpuBackend` for them — `latent-gpu/src/lib.rs:453-461`, `map_params` marks them `unreachable!`). So:
- **Cards that touch tone/saturation math (E1-C1, E1-C3, E1-C6) MUST mirror CPU↔WGSL↔reference-helper in lockstep** and keep the GPU/CPU equivalence tests green: `map_pixels_tone_matches_cpu`, `map_pixels_tone_above_one_matches_cpu`, `map_pixels_saturation_matches_cpu`, and the end-to-end `render_matches_cpu_across_the_pipeline` (all in `latent-gpu/src/lib.rs` tests). New perceptual math (L\*, LCh) added on CPU must be reproduced in WGSL.
- **Cards that touch CPU-only ops (E1-C4 curves, E1-C5 LCh mixer, E1-C7 channel mixer)** keep the equivalence test green by construction (the GPU path *is* the CPU path); each such card still states this explicitly and adds a CPU↔CPU-backend regression so the `TestBackend`/`CpuBackend` reference helper (`latent-pipeline/src/lib.rs:794+`) does not drift. If a future card ports any of these three to WGSL, the lockstep obligation transfers with it.

The `map_pixels.wgsl` `Params` struct, the `MapParams` mirror (`latent-gpu/src/lib.rs:24+`), and `map_params` (`:352`) are the CPU→GPU plumbing — any new per-op scalar (e.g. a saturation-mode flag) must be threaded through all three plus the WGSL `struct Params`.

### E1-C1 — L\* tone domain (encode/decode, re-derive shape pivots, LUT)
- Implements: `IP-03 2.1` (+ re-verify `2.4` LUT error in L\*)            Priority: High
- Crates/files: `latent-image/src/tone.rs` (`tone_encode`/`tone_decode`, `GAMMA`, `apply_linear`, the four shapes, LUT), `latent-image/src/color.rs` (consume E0-C1 `L*`), `latent-gpu/src/map_pixels.wgsl` (`tone_channel`, `Params.gamma`), `latent-gpu/src/lib.rs` (`map_params` Tone arm), `latent-pipeline/src/lib.rs` (`midtone_weight` uses `tone::GAMMA` at `:454`)
- Depends on: E0-C1 (`L*` encode/decode), E0-C3/C6 (working space settled)            Blocks: E1-C2, E1-C3, E1-C4 (all evaluate in the L\* domain)
- Heads-up:
  - **Approach.** Replace the pure γ2.2 `tone_encode`/`tone_decode` (`tone.rs:18-26`) with a **true CIE L\*** encode/decode. The tone domain is the value `t ∈ [0,1]` the curves operate on; today `t = linear^(1/2.2)`. Make `t = L*(linear)/100` (normalized so mid-gray ≈ 0.5) and decode by inverting L\* back to linear-Y-proportional light. Use the E0-C1 module's `L*` (computed via XYZ Y from the working primaries — Lab is D50-native, which is why E0's working space must be settled first). `apply_linear` becomes `decode(eval(encode(linear)))` with the new pair.
  - **Per-channel subtlety.** Today tone runs per channel on the raw channel value through γ2.2 (`apply_linear` called per channel in `PointOp::Tone`, `lib.rs:805-808`; WGSL `tone_channel` per component). Keep that per-channel application (it's the established behavior and what the GPU mirrors) — i.e. L\* here is the **L\* transfer applied to each channel independently**, not a true colorimetric L\* of the pixel. Document this clearly: it's a perceptual *encoding* swap (γ2.2 → L\* curve), not a move to operating on pixel lightness. (Operating on true pixel L\* is a different, larger change — out of scope; saturation/mixer get true LCh in C5/C6.)
  - **The pivots shift — re-derive the shapes.** The four shapes (`contrast`/`highlights`/`shadows`/`blacks`) pivot around mid-gray. In γ2.2 mid-gray (linear 0.18) encodes to ≈0.466; in L\* it lands at ≈0.5 (L\* of 0.18·white ≈ 49.6 → 0.496). The shape polynomials currently assume the pivot is wherever 0.5 maps — `contrast` pivots literally at `t=0.5` via `smoothstep`. **Re-evaluate each shape's behavior in the L\* domain** so contrast still pivots on perceptual mid-gray and highlights/shadows/blacks still target the right tonal regions. Don't just swap the encode and assume the shapes are unchanged — verify the pivot lands at L\*≈0.5 and adjust shape coefficients/windows if the visual target moved. (Note: E1-C2 replaces `contrast` entirely; coordinate the pivot there.)
  - **Why.** γ2.2 is only "comparable to" L\*'s cube root and isn't perceptually uniform, so step-uniformity and pivot placement are perceptually wrong. L\* makes the contrast/highlight/shadow/black controls perceptually correct (`tone.rs:13-16` comment currently calls γ2.2 "a reasonable perceptual space" — replace that rationale).
  - **LUT (2.4) stays, re-verify in L\*.** The 256-entry LUT is domain-agnostic and stays. Re-measure its interpolation error **in the L\* domain** (the audit's sub-LSB-at-16-bit claim was measured in γ2.2; confirm it still holds since L\* has a different curvature near black — the cube-root toe is steeper). Keep `LUT_SIZE = 256`.
  - **GPU lockstep (mandatory).** `map_pixels.wgsl` `tone_channel` hard-codes the γ2.2 `pow(x, 1/gamma)` / `pow(v, gamma)` round-trip (`:36,51`). Replace with the **same L\* encode/decode** in WGSL. `Params.gamma` may become unused or be repurposed — if L\* needs the reference-white Y (it's normalized so probably not), thread it; otherwise drop `gamma` from `Params`/`MapParams`/`map_params` consistently. The LUT upload path is unchanged (still `curve.lut()`). Keep the >1 headroom branch as-is for now (E1-C3 fixes it) but ensure the new decode passes L>1 sensibly.
  - **Coordination.** `pipeline::midtone_weight` (`lib.rs:445-455`) builds its midtone window in `tone::GAMMA` ("its peak lands on perceptual mid-gray"). If you change/remove `GAMMA`, update that helper to use the L\* encode so clarity's midtone peak still lands on perceptual mid-gray. This is an E5 concern but the symbol lives here — at minimum keep `GAMMA` exported or provide an `encode`/`decode` the helper can call.
- Acceptance:
  - Behavior: tone in L\* — mid-gray pivot at L\*≈0.5; identity preserved (zero-amount controls and the identity curve are still no-ops through the new round-trip); each shape still moves its intended tonal region.
  - Tests (rename/retune existing where the domain changed): `identity_apply_linear_is_a_noop`, `zero_amount_controls_are_identity` pass against L\*; `contrast_pushes_tones_away_from_mid` re-asserted with the pivot at L\*≈0.5 (mid-gray input maps to itself, a highlight brightens, a shadow darkens). New `tone_domain_is_lstar` asserting `encode(mid_gray_linear) ≈ 0.5` and `decode(encode(x)) ≈ x`. New `lut_error_bounded_in_lstar` re-measuring the LUT interpolation error in L\* (assert ≤ the documented bound).
  - GPU: `map_pixels_tone_matches_cpu` stays green with the L\* WGSL `tone_channel` (CPU and GPU agree within tolerance on a tone op); `render_matches_cpu_across_the_pipeline` stays green.

### E1-C2 — Always-monotone S-curve contrast
- Implements: `IP-03 2.2`            Priority: High
- Crates/files: `latent-image/src/tone.rs` (`contrast`, `smoothstep`), `latent-edit/src/lib.rs` (`SelectiveTone` — coordinate sanitize with E7-C1), tone tests
- Depends on: E1-C1 (operates in the L\* domain)            Blocks: —
- Heads-up:
  - **Approach.** Replace the smoothstep contrast (`tone.rs:94-96`, `t + amount*(smoothstep(t)-t)` clamped) with a **strictly-monotone S-curve family** whose slope is positive for **all** amounts — e.g. a **logistic / power-pivot contrast** about the perceptual mid-gray pivot (L\*≈0.5 from E1-C1). A power-pivot form: split at the pivot `p`, map `(t/p)` and `((1-t)/(1-p))` through `x^k` with `k = exp(c·amount)` (k>1 steepens = more contrast, k<1 flattens = less), rescaled to keep the pivot and endpoints fixed — `f'>0` everywhere by construction. (Logistic-around-pivot is the alternative; pick one and justify in a comment.)
  - **Why.** The smoothstep contrast **inverts** tones for `amount>1` (slope goes negative); the old fix was to clamp `amount` to `[-1,1]`, which caps the tool's range. A monotone-by-construction curve fixes the inversion **and** keeps full range — the higher-quality choice (this is the 2026-06-27 revision: clamp → always-monotone). **This replaces the clamping** — do not re-add an `amount` clamp.
  - **Still sanitize NaN/inf on load.** Monotonicity removes the need to clamp *range*, but a corrupt sidecar can still carry `NaN`/`inf` in `SelectiveTone.contrast` etc. The broad sanitize is owned by **E7-C1** (`Settings::sanitize`); this card does **not** add a second sanitize pass — just ensure the contrast math is finite-in/finite-out for finite inputs and note the dependency on E7-C1 for the load-time scrub. (If E7-C1 hasn't landed, the curve must still not panic on a finite-but-extreme amount.)
  - **Pivot.** The pivot must be the L\*-mid-gray from E1-C1 (≈0.5), not a hardcoded 0.5 that happens to be right only by coincidence — share the pivot constant with C1.
  - **GPU.** Contrast is lowered into a `ToneCurve` LUT (`tone_curves` → `PointOp::Tone`), so the GPU consumes the curve via its LUT — **no WGSL change needed**; the new shape flows through the existing tone LUT path. State this: the equivalence test (`render_matches_cpu_across_the_pipeline`) covers it because the LUT is identical CPU/GPU.
- Acceptance:
  - Behavior: contrast is strictly increasing (`f'>0`) for amounts across the full slider range including `>1` and `<-1`; never inverts; endpoints and the L\* pivot stay fixed; zero amount is identity.
  - Tests: `contrast_is_monotone_for_all_amounts` — sweep amount over e.g. `[-3, -2, …, 3]`, sample the curve densely on `[0,1]`, assert every successive output is strictly greater (the audit's Python monotonicity harness, ported). `contrast_zero_is_identity`. `contrast_pivots_at_lstar_mid` (mid-gray fixed). Optionally `contrast_extends_past_unit_amount` (asserts a stronger-than-1 amount still produces a valid monotone curve, demonstrating the range is no longer capped).

### E1-C3 — Headroom pass-through with unit slope (+ GPU mirror, fix comments)
- Implements: `IP-03 2.3`            Priority: High
- Crates/files: `latent-image/src/tone.rs` (`ToneCurve::eval` the `t>1` branch `:53-59`, and the misleading comments `:19-21,54-56,140-141`), `latent-gpu/src/map_pixels.wgsl` (`tone_channel` `>1` branch `:38-40`)
- Depends on: E1-C1 (the `>1` regime is now in L\* terms)            Blocks: —
- Heads-up:
  - **Approach.** Apply the contrast/highlights/shadows bend **only on `[0,1]`**, and pass highlight headroom (`L>1`) through with **unit slope** — i.e. for `t>1`, return `lut[last] + (t - 1.0)·1.0` (or equivalently `eval(1.0) + (t-1.0)`), not the LUT end-slope. This preserves headroom as the docs *say* they intend.
  - **The bug today.** `eval`'s `>1` branch extrapolates with the curve's **end slope** `(lut[n-1]-lut[n-2])·(n-1)`. For a contrast curve the end slope is `f'(1)≈1−a→0`, so a linear-8.0 highlight soft-clips to ~1.04 — the *opposite* of "shape, don't crush." Unit slope keeps a linear-8.0 highlight near 8.0 (modulo the L\* encode/decode round-trip, which is now the C1 L\* transfer — verify a >1 value survives encode→pass-through→decode roughly intact).
  - **Identity still passes through.** For the identity curve, end-slope and unit-slope both give 1, so identity is unaffected — but a *shaped* curve now stops bending above 1 instead of flattening. Confirm `id.apply_linear(1.5) ≈ 1.5` still holds and a contrast curve no longer crushes a >1 ramp.
  - **Fix the misleading comments.** `tone.rs:19-21` ("No upper clamp … so the curve can shape them"), `:54-56` ("extrapolate with the curve's end slope … so headroom is shaped"), and the test comment `:140-141` all describe the *wrong* (soft-clipping) behavior as if it were correct. Reword to: the bend applies on `[0,1]`; above 1, headroom passes with unit slope (shape, don't crush). The `apply_linear_preserves_headroom_and_stays_monotonic` test (`:150-168`) asserts a contrast ramp stays *increasing* — keep that, but it should now also be roughly slope-1 above 1.
  - **GPU lockstep (mandatory).** `map_pixels.wgsl` `tone_channel` (`:38-40`) mirrors the same end-slope extrapolation — change it to unit slope identically. The comment there (`:33-35`, "M4 highlight handling … extrapolate past the LUT with its end slope") must be corrected too. The reference helper / `TestBackend` tone path (`lib.rs:805-808` → `ToneCurve::eval`) inherits the fix automatically since it calls `eval`.
- Acceptance:
  - Behavior: for `t>1`, `eval` returns `eval(1) + (t-1)` (unit slope); a shaped (contrast) curve no longer soft-clips headroom; identity still passes `>1` through unchanged; below 0 still clamps to the floor.
  - Tests: `eval_passes_headroom_with_unit_slope` (replaces/augments `eval_clamps_lows_but_extrapolates_highlights`): a contrast curve maps `eval(2.0) ≈ eval(1.0) + 1.0`, **not** a flattened ~1.04. `apply_linear_preserves_headroom_*` keeps passing (and ideally asserts the >1 region is ~unit-slope after the L\* round-trip). 
  - GPU: `map_pixels_tone_above_one_matches_cpu` stays green with the unit-slope WGSL branch.

### E1-C4 — Monotone-cubic (PCHIP) `point_curve`, made total (NaN/inf-safe)
- Implements: `IP-03 2.8` + `CR-02 H1`            Priority: High (H1 is a reachable render-time panic)
- Crates/files: `latent-pipeline/src/lib.rs` (`point_curve` `:604-623`, `channel_curves` `:592-599`)
- Depends on: E1-C1 (curve operates in the L\* perceptual domain)            Blocks: —
- Heads-up:
  - **One rewrite, two halves.** This single `point_curve` rewrite covers **IP-03 2.8** (interpolation quality) **and** **CR-02 H1** (panic safety) — the registers explicitly say "do them in one edit" (same function, `pipeline:604-623`).
  - **Interpolation upgrade (2.8).** Replace the C0 piecewise-linear interpolation (`:618-621`, `y0 + (t-x0)/(x1-x0)*(y1-y0)`) with **monotonicity-preserving cubic** — **Fritsch–Carlson / PCHIP**: compute secant slopes between control points, derive per-point tangents, then **limit the tangents** (Fritsch–Carlson) so no overshoot/oscillation occurs — guaranteeing the spline is monotone wherever the control points are monotone (unlike plain Catmull–Rom, which can overshoot/invert). Evaluate the Hermite cubic on the bracketing segment. The result is C1-smooth (no slope kinks at control points) and shape-preserving.
  - **Make it total (H1 — the panic).** Confirmed reachable panic at `:618`: `total_cmp` orders `NaN` **last**, so the high-end guard `t >= pts[last].0` is `t >= NaN == false` and never fires; then `windows(2).position(|w| t <= w[1].0)` returns `None` for `t` past the largest *real* x and `.unwrap()` crashes — at render time, since `ToneCurve::from_fn` samples across `[0,1]`. A `NaN` reaches here trivially (RON accepts `NaN`/`inf` verbatim → corrupt/hand-edited sidecar = DoS on opening the editor). Fix, in this order, inside `point_curve`:
    1. **Drop non-finite control points** (`x` or `y` not finite) before sorting.
    2. **Clamp** each remaining `x` and `y` to `[0,1]`.
    3. If **empty after the drop**, return `ToneCurve::identity()` (empty-is-identity preserved).
    4. Sort by `x` (`total_cmp` now safe — all finite).
    5. Replace the fragile `.position(...).unwrap()` with a **saturating search** that can never return `None` (e.g. `.unwrap_or(last - 1)`, or a manual clamp of the segment index). The earlier-window selection at duplicate x is **verified not a divide-by-zero** (the `t <= w[1].0` search picks the earlier window, never a zero-width segment) — keep that property; for PCHIP, guard any zero-width segment (`x1==x0`) defensively anyway (skip / treat as a step) since clamping can now collapse points.
  - **Keep these behaviors.** Clamp-flat-past-ends (`t<=pts[0].0 → pts[0].1`, `t>=pts[last].0 → pts[last].1`) and empty-is-identity. The composition order in `channel_curves` (`channel ∘ master`, master-then-channel — verified correct in 2.8) is **unchanged**.
  - **Defense in depth, not redundancy.** This makes the function total even if a caller mis-sets a point *programmatically after load*; **E7-C1**'s `Settings::sanitize` is the complementary load-time scrub (IP-03 2.2 defers to it). Both are wanted — note the E7-C1 dependency for the load layer but implement the total function regardless of E7-C1's status.
  - **CPU-only / equivalence.** `Curves`/`Matrix`/`ColorMix` run on the CPU in both backends (GPU falls back — `lib.rs:455`), so `point_curve` has no WGSL mirror; the equivalence test stays green by construction. State this and add the CPU regression tests below so the `TestBackend`↔`CpuBackend` curve path (both call `point_curve`) doesn't drift.
- Acceptance:
  - Behavior: monotone control points → monotone spline (no overshoot between points); endpoints matched exactly; flat past the ends; empty → identity; **no panic and finite output** for any input including `NaN`/`inf`/out-of-range x or y.
  - Tests (in `latent-pipeline`): `point_curve_monotone_inputs_stay_monotone` (rising control points → densely-sampled output strictly non-decreasing, and a known monotone set shows no overshoot vs the linear baseline). `point_curve_matches_endpoints`. `point_curve_empty_is_identity`. `point_curve_single_point` (flat). `point_curve_unsorted_input` (sorted internally). `point_curve_duplicate_x_stays_finite` (regression — pins the not-a-divide-by-zero property). **`point_curve_nan_inf_points_no_panic`** (NaN/inf in x or y → dropped, finite output, no panic — the H1 regression).

### E1-C6 — Chroma-preserving saturation (LCh)
- Implements: `IP-03 2.7` (supersedes the Audit-01 #5 luma-blend idea, T2)            Priority: Medium
- Crates/files: `latent-pipeline/src/lib.rs` (`PointOp::Saturation` lowering `:386-388`, `TestBackend` reference `:810-817`), `latent-cpu` backend Saturation impl, `latent-gpu/src/map_pixels.wgsl` (op 2 `:74-78`), `latent-gpu/src/lib.rs` (`map_params` Saturation arm `:374-377`), consume E0-C1 Lab/LCh
- Depends on: E0-C1 (Lab/LCh + inverse)            Blocks: —
- Heads-up:
  - **Approach.** Replace the luma-blend saturation (`y + amount·(channel − y)` about Rec.709-ish working luma) with a **chroma-preserving** op: convert working→Lab→LCh (E0-C1), **scale chroma `C` by `amount` at constant L\* and hue**, then back. Desaturation (`amount→0`) collapses to **mid-gray at the same lightness** (C→0), and over-saturation (`amount>1`) grows chroma without shifting lightness. Clamp the resulting working RGB to ≥0 as today (the `.max(0.0)` over-saturation guard) — and keep it in both backends.
  - **Why.** The luma-blend uses `LUMA_WEIGHTS` whose **blue weight is ~0.0001** (wide ProPhoto primaries — `color.rs:181`), so desaturating a pure blue lerps it toward near-**black**, not gray. Constant-L\* chroma scaling fixes that: lightness is held while chroma moves. **This supersedes the luma-blend entirely** — the separate "use Rec.709 luma for saturation" idea from Audit-01 #5 is **dropped** (T2): saturation uses LCh chroma, **no luma blend, no `LUMA_WEIGHTS` in the saturation path**. (`LUMA_WEIGHTS` may still exist for other consumers; just not here.)
  - **Three backends in lockstep (mandatory — this is a `Backend` primitive).** Update all three: (1) `TestBackend::map_pixels` Saturation arm (`lib.rs:810-817`, the reference helper); (2) the real `CpuBackend` Saturation impl in `latent-cpu`; (3) the **WGSL** op 2 (`map_pixels.wgsl:74-78`). Porting Lab/LCh to WGSL is the substantive part: the XYZ↔Lab cube-root/`f(t)` transfer and the working↔XYZ matrices must be **mirrored in the shader** (the same constants E0-C1 defines on CPU — hardcode them in WGSL as `LUMA_WEIGHTS` is today, with a comment pinning them to the CPU source).
